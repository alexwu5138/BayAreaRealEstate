# ZIP Code Report: 95120 (Almaden Valley, San Jose)

## 1. Geological & Physical Setting
### 1.1 Geographic Position
ZIP code **95120** covers most of **Almaden Valley**, on the southwestern edge of San Jose, California. It is nestled against the **Santa Teresa Hills** and **Sierra Azul foothills**, with residential areas extending up toward the open-space preserves.

### 1.2 Natural Boundaries & Topography
The area is characterized by **rolling foothills, canyon-like valleys, and elevated view lots**. Natural edges include the **Almaden Quicksilver County Park** ridgeline, hills along Hicks Road, and creek corridors such as **Guadalupe Creek** and **Los Alamitos Creek**. Compared to central San Jose, the terrain is more varied, with noticeable elevation changes and hillside streets.

### 1.3 Land Use Patterns
Land use is **predominantly low‑density residential** (detached single‑family homes), with:
- Neighborhood and community shopping centers along **Almaden Expressway**, **Camden Avenue**, and **Branham Lane**.
- Parks, greenbelts, and school campuses distributed throughout.
- Very limited industrial or office space inside the ZIP; most residents commute out to regional job hubs.

## 2. Climate & Environmental Hazards
### 2.1 Local Climate & Weather Patterns
Almaden Valley has an **inland Mediterranean climate**:
- **Hot, dry summers** (many days in the high 80s–90s°F in July–August).
- **Mild, wet winters**, with cooler overnight temperatures than central San Jose due to valley and foothill effects.
- Air quality is generally good to moderate, but sensitive to seasonal wildfire smoke.

### 2.2 Wildfire Exposure & Historical Fire Activity
The ZIP includes **foothill and hillside areas near wildland vegetation**. While large parts of the residential area are not in the most extreme State‑mapped Very High Fire Hazard Severity Zones, **wildfire and smoke are material considerations**:
- Hillside streets and canyon neighborhoods have higher exposure.
- Regional wildfires in the South Bay and Santa Cruz Mountains can periodically cause poor air quality and heightened awareness of evacuation planning.

### 2.3 Flood, Stormwater, and Water‑Related Hazards
Most of 95120 sits well above the valley floor, so **classic riverine flooding is less dominant** than in lower San Jose, but:
- Certain parcels close to **creeks and drainage channels** have localized stormwater/flood potential.
- Heavier winter storms can cause minor roadway flooding and saturated soils, especially along low‑lying crossings.

### 2.4 Seismic Activity & Fault Proximity
Like the rest of the Bay Area, Almaden Valley is subject to **regional seismic risk**:
- Influenced by nearby major faults (e.g., **San Andreas**, **Calaveras**, and related systems).
- Most homes are conventional wood‑frame construction that historically performs better than unreinforced masonry, but **retrofit status is parcel‑specific**.

### 2.5 Environmental Compliance, Superfund Sites & Pollution Indicators
There are **no major Superfund sites within 95120** itself. Environmental concerns are mostly:
- **Air quality impacts** from regional wildfire smoke.
- Routine urban issues (traffic emissions, residential pesticide use).
Overall, the area is perceived as **clean, low‑pollution, and nature‑oriented** by residents.

## 3. Community & Demographics
### 3.1 Communities, Neighborhoods, and Subdivisions
95120 is commonly referred to simply as **Almaden Valley** and includes:
- Established single‑family subdivisions near **Almaden Lake**, **Camden Ave**, and **Almaden Expressway**.
- More **upscale hillside and view neighborhoods** along **Hicks Road**, **Montego**, **Pierce Ranch**, **Shadow Brook**, and other named tracts.
- Many micro‑neighborhoods organized around elementary schools (Williams, Graystone, Los Alamitos, Simonds, Guadalupe).

The overall feel is **scenic, low‑density, and strongly family‑oriented**.

### 3.2 Population Size, Density & Demographic Composition
Demographic estimates vary with the exact boundary used (ZIP vs. neighborhood definition), but current data indicates:
- Population on the order of **38k–47k residents**.
- **Median age in the high 40s**, reflecting many established households and fewer young renters.
- High share of **families with children** and **owner‑occupied homes**.

Ethnic composition is diverse, with substantial representation from professional households across multiple backgrounds, consistent with broader high‑skill South Bay patterns.

### 3.3 Household Characteristics (Age, Education, Household Size)
Recent ACS‑based demographic sources report:
- **Median household income** in the **low‑ to mid‑$200k range**, with some estimates around or above \$220k–\$250k.
- **Average household income** comfortably over \$300k in some estimates.
- High educational attainment, with a large share of adults holding **bachelor’s and graduate degrees**.
- Household size is typically **3–4 persons**, consistent with family households.

Overall, Almaden Valley is an **affluent, highly educated, predominantly homeowner community**.

## 4. Housing & Neighborhood Character
### 4.1 Housing Stock Distribution & Architectural Character
Key traits of the 95120 housing stock:
- Primarily **detached single‑family homes**, many built from the 1970s onward, with continuing waves of remodels and expansions.
- **Architectural style** ranges from classic ranch and two‑story tract homes to custom hillside properties with views, pools, and more contemporary finishes.
- Limited but present **townhome and condo complexes**, mostly closer to main corridors and lake/expressway areas.

### 4.2 Median Home Prices, Trends & Affordability Metrics
As of late 2025, 95120 remains on the **higher‑end of South Bay pricing**:
- **Median sale price** is around **\$2.2M**, with median price per square foot close to **\$1,000/sq ft**.
- Median **list price** is reported in the **\$2.1M–\$2.2M** range, with a sale‑to‑list ratio near 1.0 and **roughly 11–15 days to pending** for typical listings.
- Year‑over‑year price movement is **slightly down or flat in nominal terms** but sitting on top of very large gains since 2019.

Market characterization:
- **Somewhat to highly competitive**: multiple offers (around ~5 on average is common in recent Redfin data).
- Inventory is **limited** (few dozen active listings), so **buyers compete for well‑located, updated homes**, especially in premium school pockets.

### 4.3 Income Distribution & Median Household Income
Income levels are consistent with high Silicon Valley employment:
- Recent neighborhood‑level data shows **median household incomes well above \$200k**, significantly exceeding California and U.S. medians.
- Per‑capita income and average individual income are also elevated, reflecting a large concentration of **engineers, tech professionals, managers, and medical/financial professionals**.

### 4.4 Rental Market Snapshot
The area is **overwhelmingly owner‑occupied**:
- Rental stock is relatively limited and often consists of **single‑family homes held as investment properties** or **higher‑end townhomes**.
- Rents are **substantially above U.S. averages**, and properties with access to top schools command a premium.
- For tenants, 95120 is generally positioned as a **“lifestyle” and “school‑driven”** choice, not a budget option.

## 5. Commute & Access
### 5.1 Major Roadways, Transit Access & Walkability
Primary transportation elements:
- **Almaden Expressway** and **Highway 85** form the backbone for commuting to other parts of San Jose and Silicon Valley.
- Public transit is **limited**: VTA bus routes exist, but there is **no Caltrain or BART station within 95120**. Most residents rely on driving.
- Walkability is **neighborhood‑centric**: residents often can walk to local schools and parks, but cross‑ZIP errands typically require a car.

### 5.2 Typical Commute Times to Key Employment Areas
Indicative one‑way drive times in normal (non‑peak) traffic:
- **Downtown San Jose / Diridon Station**: ~25–30 minutes.
- **North San Jose / Cisco area**: ~30–40 minutes.
- **Cupertino / Apple Park**: ~30–40 minutes via 85.
- **Mountain View / Google**: ~35–50 minutes depending on 85/280 traffic.

In peak commute periods, these times can **stretch to 45–60+ minutes**, especially toward the north Peninsula and core Silicon Valley job centers.

### 5.3 Infrastructure Constraints & Planned Improvements
Within 95120 itself:
- The biggest constraint is **high dependence on driving**, with **rush‑hour congestion on 85 and feeder arterials**.
- City‑level planning shows ongoing **major projects in greater San Jose**, but no transformative new transit line entering Almaden Valley in the near term.
- Local roadway and safety upgrades are incremental (signal timing, bike lane additions, resurfacing), not structural game‑changers.

## 7. Safety
### 7.1 Overall Crime Levels
Recent crime‑mapping and scoring tools rate 95120 as **well above average in safety**:
- Overall crime rate is on the order of **10 incidents per 1,000 residents per year**, which is **low compared to both national and many Bay Area norms**.
- Cost‑of‑crime analyses estimate **per‑resident and per‑household crime‑related costs** significantly below citywide San Jose averages.

### 7.2 Violent vs. Property Crime
Breakdown by category:
- **Violent crime rates** are **well below U.S. averages**, with neighborhood‑level chances of being a victim of violent crime often in the range of **1 in several hundred** residents per year.
- **Property crime** (theft, burglary) is more common than violent crime but still relatively low compared with other urban ZIPs.

In short, Almaden is perceived, and statistically supported, as one of **San Jose’s safer areas**.

### 7.3 Crime Trend & Recent Incidents
Citywide San Jose data for 2025 shows:
- **Violent crime down double digits year‑over‑year**, and
- **Property crime down single digits year‑over‑year**,
which aligns with a general **downward trend in major incidents**.

At the micro level:
- There have been **periodic spikes in residential burglaries** in certain Almaden neighborhoods, prompting **community meetings, neighborhood watch programs, and, in some cases, private security patrols**.
- These are important for risk perception but do not erase the area’s overall **strong safety profile**.

### 7.4 Localized Concerns
Localized concerns that sometimes surface in resident discussions:
- **Package theft and vehicle break‑ins** near shopping centers and high‑traffic areas.
- Occasional **clusters of home burglaries**, usually followed by increased enforcement and resident vigilance.
Overall, however, Almaden Valley remains widely viewed as **safe to walk, jog, and raise a family**, especially compared to many urban cores.

## 8. Schools & Education
### 8.1 Elementary Schools
Most of 95120 feeds into **San Jose Unified School District (SJUSD)**. Key elementary schools commonly associated with Almaden Valley include:
- **Williams Elementary** – High proficiency scores in both Math and ELA, with student performance significantly above California averages.
- **Graystone Elementary** – Similarly strong academic outcomes, with many grade levels showing ~80% or better proficiency in core subjects.
- **Los Alamitos Elementary** – Large TK–5 campus with solid performance and strong community involvement.
- **Simonds Elementary** – Stable enrollment, good outcomes, and close integration with surrounding neighborhoods.
- **Guadalupe Elementary** – Serves the southern/foothill part of 95120, with performance at or above district averages.

Class sizes generally fall in the **low‑20s per teacher**, which is favorable by California standards.

### 8.2 Middle Schools
The primary middle school for 95120 is:
- **Bret Harte Middle School** – Frequently cited as one of the **top middle schools in SJUSD**, with strong standardized test performance and a reputation for high‑achieving students and robust extracurricular offerings.

Some fringe pockets may feed to **Castillero Middle School** (just outside the core ZIP), depending on the exact attendance boundaries.

### 8.3 High Schools
The flagship high school for 95120 is:
- **Leland High School (SJUSD)** – A **top‑tier public high school**:
  - Rated **10/10 on major school‑rating platforms**.
  - Consistently in the **top few percent of California public high schools** by test‑score‑based rankings.
  - Very strong **college‑prep profile**, with:
    - Dozens of **AP and honors courses**.
    - High **AP participation and pass rates**.
    - **Graduation rates in the mid‑ to high‑90% range**.
  - Recent rankings (2024–2025) continue to place Leland among the **top high schools in the state** for overall academics and for STEM.

Some edges of the ZIP feed to **Pioneer High School**, which still offers solid programs but does not carry the same Almaden‑brand recognition as Leland.

### 8.4 Private, Charter & Alternative Educational Options
Within or near 95120, families often also consider:
- **Almaden Country Day School (PK–8)** – Independent school with low student‑to‑teacher ratios and a strong reputation; tuition priced at the higher independent‑school tier.
- **Holy Spirit School (TK–8, Catholic)** – Parochial school serving local parish families with a long community history.
- **Challenger School – Almaden (PK–8)** – Private school emphasizing academic rigor and structured curriculum.
Additional private high schools (e.g., Archbishop Mitty, Bellarmine, Presentation/Notre Dame, Harker) are reachable by car or shuttle from Almaden.

### 8.5 School Performance Metrics & Boundary Considerations
Key practical implications for buyers, sellers, and residents:
- **Attendance boundaries matter**: Being **inside** the boundary for Williams/Graystone/Bret Harte/Leland versus just outside can significantly impact both perceived value and buyer demand.
- **Performance consistency**: The **K–8 pipeline feeding Leland** has demonstrated **consistent academic strength over many years**, not just in a single rating cycle.
- **Class size and campus capacity**: Elementaries typically range from ~450–600 students; high school enrollment at Leland usually sits in the **1,400–1,600 student range**, with robust program breadth but still manageable campus size.

From a real‑estate perspective, 95120’s school ecosystem—especially the **Williams/Graystone → Bret Harte → Leland** path—is one of the **primary drivers of the ZIP’s sustained premium pricing and buyer interest**.
