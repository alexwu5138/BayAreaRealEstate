# ZIP Code 95008 — Campbell, California
*Santa Clara County | Central-West Valley | Downtown-Oriented Community with Mixed Housing Types*

## 1. Geological & Physical Setting

### 1.1 Geographic Position
ZIP **95008** corresponds primarily to **Campbell**, located between West San José, Los Gatos, and Santa Clara/Sunnyvale access corridors. It benefits from strong connectivity to the South Bay job network and has an identifiable downtown core.

### 1.2 Natural Boundaries & Topography
- Mostly flat valley terrain with some gentle grade changes.
- Drainage is largely engineered; localized creek/drainage features can influence specific blocks.

### 1.3 Land Use Patterns
- Mixed residential: single-family tracts + townhomes/condos + multifamily nodes.
- Active downtown/village commercial environment and corridor retail/services.
- Built-out, with infill and redevelopment pressure in select corridors.

---

## 2. Climate & Environmental Hazards

### 2.1 Climate
Warm summers and mild winters; typical Santa Clara Valley patterns.

### 2.2 Wildfire
Generally low wildfire exposure versus foothill/WUI communities, though buyers should still verify address-level hazard designations if near open-space edges.

### 2.3 Flood/Stormwater
Localized stormwater issues can exist; address-level FEMA check is standard practice. Evaluate drainage, grading, and any history of yard/garage flooding.

### 2.4 Seismic
Baseline Bay Area seismic exposure; diligence focuses on foundation condition and retrofit history for older homes.

### 2.5 Environmental
Primarily residential/retail with limited heavy industry; rely on disclosures for any site-specific concerns.

## 8. Schools & Education
School assignment is address-based and may involve Campbell Union School District (elementary/middle) and Campbell Union High School District / other high school districts depending on boundaries. Verify assignment for any specific property.

## Practical Due-Diligence Checklist (95008)
1. Verify school assignment by exact address.
2. Confirm FEMA flood zone; evaluate drainage history.
3. For older homes, evaluate electrical/plumbing updates, sewer laterals, and permits.
4. For condos/townhomes: HOA reserves, special assessments, litigation, and rental restrictions.
5. Price downtown proximity (amenity premium) versus noise/parking tradeoffs.
