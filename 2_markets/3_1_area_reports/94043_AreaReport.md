# ZIP Code Report: 94043 (North Mountain View – North Whisman / Shoreline / Moffett Area)

## 1. Geological & Physical Setting
### 1.1 Geographic Position
ZIP code **94043** covers much of **north and northeast Mountain View**, including neighborhoods around **North Whisman, Monta Loma, Rex Manor, Jackson Park, and parts of North Bayshore/Moffett area**. It sits in the flat northern Santa Clara Valley, very close to the San Francisco Bay shoreline and adjacent to U.S. 101.

### 1.2 Natural Boundaries & Topography
Topography is **flat alluvial plain**, with:
- The **Bayfront / Marshes** to the north and northeast.
- The **Stevens Creek and Permanente Creek** drainage systems influencing stormwater design.
- The **Moffett Federal Airfield / NASA Ames** complex anchoring the north boundary.
Elevation is low, making tidal and creek‑related flooding a planning consideration in some areas.

### 1.3 Land Use Patterns
94043 is a **mixed residential–employment ZIP**:
- Substantial **single‑family neighborhoods** in Monta Loma, Rex Manor, and Jackson Park.
- Significant **tech‑oriented office/R&D campuses** in North Whisman and near Shoreline/North Bayshore.
- Multi‑family housing (apartments, condos) close to employment cores and transit corridors.
This blend gives 94043 a more **urban and job‑rich** character than many purely residential ZIPs.

## 2. Climate & Environmental Hazards
### 2.1 Local Climate & Weather Patterns
- Typical **Peninsula/Bay microclimate**: milder than inland South Bay, with more marine influence.
- Summers warm but often moderated by afternoon bay breezes; winters cool and damp.
- Air quality generally **Good–Moderate**, with some freeway and tech‑corridor emissions.

### 2.2 Wildfire Exposure & Historical Fire Activity
94043 is **far from forested foothills**, so direct wildfire risk is low, but:
- Regional wildfire smoke (from Santa Cruz Mountains, North Bay, Sierra) can still degrade AQI several days per year.

### 2.3 Flood, Stormwater, and Water‑Related Hazards
- Being close to the Bay and at low elevation, some parts of 94043 are **exposed to creek and tidal flooding** under extreme storm/sea‑level scenarios.
- Local stormwater projects have been ongoing, but **flood‑zone and sea‑level‑rise mapping** remains relevant for certain parcels and new development.

### 2.4 Seismic Activity & Fault Proximity
The ZIP shares the **Bay Area’s regional seismic risk**, influenced by:
- Nearby **San Andreas Fault** to the west and other regional faults.
- Mostly wood‑frame residential and steel/RC office structures that are generally better than masonry but still require code‑compliant design and retrofit.

### 2.5 Environmental Compliance, Superfund Sites & Pollution Indicators
- 94043 includes **long‑standing tech and aerospace R&D land**, some of which has undergone environmental remediation (e.g., former industrial sites around Moffett/NASA zones).
- Modern projects are subject to **stringent environmental review**. For typical residential buyers, concerns are more about **traffic/air quality and noise** than legacy contamination, but specific parcels near historical industrial sites may warrant extra due diligence.

## 3. Community & Demographics
### 3.1 Communities, Neighborhoods, and Subdivisions
Key residential neighborhoods in 94043 include:
- **Monta Loma** – mid‑century homes, strong community identity, close to tech offices.
- **Rex Manor / Jackson Park** – smaller lots, more urban feel, a mix of SFH and multifamily.
- **North Whisman** – blend of apartments, townhomes, and tech campuses.
- Portions of **North Bayshore** – historically office‑heavy with limited housing, but targeted for more mixed‑use over time.

### 3.2 Population Size, Density & Demographic Composition
Recent ACS/ZIP sources report:  
- **Population:** ≈ **32,000** residents.  
- **Land area:** ~8.5 sq mi; **density ~3,700–3,800 people/sq mi**, lower than many South Bay core ZIPs but higher in the purely residential tracts. 
- **Race/ethnicity:** roughly **White ~38–41%, Asian ~33–37%, Hispanic ~18–19%**, plus smaller Black and multiracial groups.

### 3.3 Household Characteristics (Age, Education, Household Size)
- **Median age:** around **36 years**, slightly younger than the California median.  
- **Median household income:** ≈ **$180–181k**, ~20% above the broader SJ‑Sunnyvale‑Santa Clara metro median.  
- Very high educational attainment: **~70%+ adults with bachelor’s degree or higher**.   
- Average household size ≈ **2.4 people**, indicating a mix of families and high‑earning singles/couples.   

Niche ranks 94043 highly for **families, livability, and public schools**, placing it in the top tier of California ZIPs.

## 4. Housing & Neighborhood Character
### 4.1 Housing Stock Distribution & Architectural Character
Housing mix includes:
- **Single‑family ranch and mid‑century modern homes** in Monta Loma and adjoining tracts.
- **Townhomes and low‑rise condos** near Whisman Station and major tech campuses.
- **Apartments and mixed‑use** buildings closer to employment corridors and transit.

The architecture is more varied and somewhat denser than typical suburban tract-only ZIPs, reflecting proximity to strong job centers.

### 4.2 Median Home Prices, Trends & Affordability Metrics
Latest housing market data (Q3–Q4 2025):
- **Redfin:** October 2025 median sale price ≈ **$1.6M**, down about **6.4% YoY**, with homes selling in ~15 days on market. 
- **Zillow:** median sale ≈ **$1.57M**, median list ≈ **$1.47M**, ~14 median days to pending, sale‑to‑list ratio ~1.02. 
- **Realtor.com:** September 2025 median listing ≈ **$1.5M**, flat year‑over‑year, with list PPSF around **$1,000/sq ft**. 

Market characterization:
- Still a **high‑priced West‑Coast tech‑hub ZIP**, but 2025 has seen **some softening** vs 2024 peak prices.
- Short DOM and strong sale‑to‑list ratios show that **well‑priced homes near jobs and transit still move quickly**.

### 4.3 Income Distribution & Median Household Income
- **Median household income ~ $181k**, with many households significantly above that level. 
- A large proportion of residents work in **high‑compensation tech roles**, pulling average earnings over **$110k+ per worker**.  

### 4.4 Rental Market Snapshot
- 94043 has a **meaningful share of renters**, especially in apartments and townhomes close to Whisman and North Bayshore.
- Rents are **very high by national standards**, often comparable to or above central Mountain View/94040 for newer buildings, but some older stock offers slightly more approachable entry points.
- Many renters are **tech workers** seeking a short commute to campuses in Mountain View, Sunnyvale, and North San Jose.

## 5. Commute & Access
### 5.1 Major Roadways, Transit Access & Walkability
Key access elements:
- Immediate proximity to **U.S. 101** and **State Route 85/237 connections**, enabling quick access to Sunnyvale, Santa Clara, San Jose, and the Peninsula.
- **Caltrain access** via **Downtown Mountain View Station** (just southwest of the ZIP), plus **VTA light rail** and multiple bus routes.
- Bicycle infrastructure (Bay Trail segments, separated paths) supports **bike commuting** to nearby tech campuses.

Walkability varies:
- Residential pockets near Castro/downtown and near certain office cores are fairly walkable; more office‑heavy parts of North Bayshore are car/bike‑or shuttle‑oriented.

### 5.2 Typical Commute Times to Key Employment Areas
Indicative drive times in normal (non‑peak) traffic:
- **Google, Intuit, other MV campuses (within 94043 itself):** ~5–15 minutes or bikeable.
- **Sunnyvale / Yahoo / LinkedIn areas:** ~10–20 minutes.
- **Santa Clara / NVIDIA / 49ers area:** ~15–25 minutes.
- **North San Jose / Cisco:** ~15–25 minutes via 237/101.
- **San Francisco (downtown) via 101 or Caltrain:** 35–60 minutes depending on mode and time of day.

### 5.3 Infrastructure Constraints & Planned Improvements
- Key constraints are **101/85/237 congestion** and peak‑hour backups at major interchanges.
- Mountain View and regional agencies continue to plan **transit, bike, and mixed‑use improvements** in North Bayshore and around the transit center, with an eye toward reducing car dependence and adding housing near jobs.

## 6. Economic Development & Outlook
### 6.1 Major Employers Within This ZIP
Unlike many residential ZIPs, 94043 contains **significant employment clusters**, including:
- Portions of **Google’s global headquarters footprint** (North Bayshore / Shoreline).
- Other major tech and R&D employers along **Whisman and Shoreline corridors**.
- The **NASA Ames Research Center / Moffett Federal Airfield** complex just across ZIP boundaries, influencing the broader jobshed.

### 6.2 Trends or Outlook
- 94043 remains a **core Silicon Valley job center**, even as some tech firms adjust office footprints in a hybrid‑work era.
- Housing demand is **tightly coupled** to tech employment cycles; 2025 has seen some price softening, but employment fundamentals remain strong.
- Regional forecasts call for **modest national home‑price growth** through 2026, with high‑cost tech markets like Mountain View expected to grow slowly but remain extremely expensive. 

### 6.3 Development Trends, Pipeline Projects & Market Outlook
- **North Bayshore and North Whisman** remain focal points for **mixed‑use and higher‑density development**, including housing near large tech campuses, though entitlements and phasing evolve over time. 
- The city is balancing **job‑housing ratios, traffic, and environmental constraints**, with an emphasis on transit‑oriented and bikeable growth rather than sprawl.
- Overall, 94043’s long‑term outlook is tied to its role as a **high‑value “jobs + housing” hub** in central Silicon Valley.

### 6.4 Resident Sentiment & Public Opinion Indicators
- Niche ranks 94043 among the **top ZIP codes in California** for families, public schools, and overall quality of life.   
- Residents often cite **proximity to jobs, transit, and downtown Mountain View** as key positives; concerns center on **housing costs, congestion, and school‑boundary nuance**.

## 7. Safety
### 7.1 Overall Crime Levels
Crime‑mapping sources show **higher crime rates than quiet suburban ZIPs**, but still better than many U.S. urban cores:
- CrimeGrade estimates a **total crime rate ~40.9 incidents per 1,000 residents per year**. 
- The projected **2025 cost of crime ≈ $16.45M**, or **~$501 per resident / $1,235 per household**, about **0.5% of median household income**. 

### 7.2 Violent vs. Property Crime
- **Violent crime:** relatively low in absolute terms but concentrated in certain micro‑areas; 2025 projections put violent‑crime cost around **$540 per household**. 
- **Property crime:** more common (theft, vehicle break‑ins, burglary), especially near commercial strips, transit hubs, and large parking lots.

### 7.3 Crime Trend & Recent Incidents
- Like many Bay Area cities, Mountain View has seen some **post‑pandemic fluctuations** in property crime, with periods of increased theft and auto break‑ins, but overall serious violent crime remains comparatively low.
- The 2024–2025 pattern in regional major‑city data shows **declining homicides and some stabilization** in serious crimes, though nuisance/property incidents remain a concern.

### 7.4 Neighborhood‑Level Safety Concerns
Risk is **highly localized** within 94043:
- Residents perceive the **southwest parts of the ZIP** (closer to Castro/downtown residential tracts) as safer, while some **northern industrial/office areas** see more reported incidents. 
- Common concerns: package theft, bike theft, car break‑ins near large apartment complexes and office parking.
Many neighborhoods maintain active **online groups and neighborhood‑watch style coordination**.

## 8. Schools & Education
### 8.1 Elementary Schools
94043 is primarily served by **Mountain View Whisman School District (MVWSD)**. Key elementaries associated with 94043 include:

- **Monta Loma Elementary** – GreatSchools currently around **6/10** (average performance); diverse student body, GATE options. 
- **Theuerkauf Elementary** – GreatSchools **4/10**, below average on test metrics but also serving a higher‑needs population. 
- **Stevenson Elementary** (choice/lottery‑based, in city but often considered by 94043 families) – GreatSchools **9/10**, strong academics and project‑based learning model.  
- Other MVWSD elementaries (e.g., Gabriela Mistral, Bubb) are nearby and may be options depending on assignment and choice programs. 

### 8.2 Middle Schools
The primary middle school serving 94043 is:
- **Crittenden Middle School** – GreatSchools **8/10**, Niche grade **A**, ~50% math and 64% reading proficiency, ~17:1 student‑teacher ratio. 

Other MVWSD middle schools (e.g., Graham) are also in the district but typically serve other attendance zones.

### 8.3 High Schools
For high school, 94043 feeds into **Mountain View–Los Altos Union High School District (MVLA UHSD)**, with the dominant assignment being:
- **Los Altos High School** – GreatSchools **10/10**, consistently one of the **top public high schools in California**; strong academics, AP offerings, and college‑prep outcomes.

Some addresses may feed to **Mountain View High School**, which likewise carries **very strong ratings** (9–10/10 range) and rigorous academic programs. 

### 8.4 Private, Charter & Alternative Educational Options
In and around 94043, families also look at:
- Various **private K–8 and high schools** across Mountain View, Los Altos, Palo Alto, and Sunnyvale (e.g., Khan Lab School, German International School, St. Francis HS, etc. – outside the ZIP but commute‑feasible).
- Charter and magnet options within MVWSD and MVLA that may be accessible via application/lottery.

### 8.5 School Performance Metrics & Boundary Considerations
Key practical points:
- **Elementary boundaries matter**: there is a notable spread between Theuerkauf (4/10) and Stevenson (9/10), so exact parcel assignments can significantly affect perceived desirability. 
- **Middle + High combination** (Crittenden + Los Altos/MVHS) is a major value driver; MVLA is widely considered a **top‑tier high‑school district** in California.
- Many buyers willing to accept **smaller homes or attached product** in 94043 in exchange for **short commutes and access to MVWSD+MVLA schools**, especially Los Altos High.

From a real‑estate perspective, 94043 offers a **rare blend of: (1) proximity to major tech campuses, (2) strong high‑school district, and (3) evolving, mixed‑use urban environment**, albeit with trade‑offs on noise, traffic, and elementary‑school variability.
