# ZIP Code 95014 — Cupertino, California
(Santa Clara County | Core West Valley Tech Hub)

## 1. Geological & Physical Setting

### Geographic Position
ZIP code 95014 covers the central and western portions of Cupertino, extending from the valley floor near Wolfe Road westward toward the lower foothills of the Santa Cruz Mountains. It borders Los Altos (94024), Sunnyvale (94087), and San José (95129).

### Topography & Natural Features
The eastern portion is generally flat former orchard land, while the western portion gently rises toward the foothills. Stevens Creek and Calabazas Creek traverse the area, both heavily flood-controlled.

### Land Use Patterns
Predominantly single-family residential with limited multifamily housing. Major office and R&D campuses are present, including Apple Park and the former Vallco area.

---

## 2. Climate & Environmental Hazards

### Climate
Warm, dry summers and mild winters with less marine influence than Peninsula cities.

### Wildfire Risk
Overall low to moderate wildfire exposure, with slightly elevated risk near western hillside edges.

### Flood & Water Considerations
Localized FEMA flood zones near creeks; no widespread floodplain risk.

### Seismic Activity
Typical South Bay seismic exposure; lower liquefaction risk than bay-adjacent ZIP codes.

### Environmental Factors
No major Superfund sites. Historic orchard-era pesticide legacy is known and managed.

---

## 3. Community & Demographics

### Neighborhoods
Includes Monta Vista, Rancho Rinconada, Homestead, Cupertino Village, and areas surrounding Apple Park.

### Population Profile
Highly educated, tech-oriented population with strong owner occupancy and low turnover.

### Household Characteristics
Predominantly family households with high dual-income professional representation.

---

## 4. Housing & Neighborhood Character

### Housing Stock
Primarily 1960s–1970s single-family ranch homes on 6,000–9,000 sq ft lots. Significant remodeling and rebuild activity.

### Pricing & Market Position
Median single-family home prices typically range from $3.0M to $3.6M, heavily influenced by school assignment.

### Income & Affordability
Among the highest household incomes in Santa Clara County; affordability constrained even for high earners.

### Rental Market
Strong executive rental demand. Single-family rents commonly range from $6,000 to $9,000+ per month.

---

## 5. Commute & Access

### Transportation
Excellent freeway access via I-280 and SR-85. Limited rail access; car-centric commute patterns.

### Commute Times
Apple Park: 5–10 minutes  
Google (Mountain View): 15–25 minutes  
Palo Alto: 20–30 minutes  
Downtown San José: 20–25 minutes

### Infrastructure Constraints
Traffic congestion during peak commute and school hours; limited roadway expansion capacity.

---

## 6. Economic Development & Outlook

### Major Employers
Apple Inc. headquarters (Apple Park) anchors the local economy, with proximity to Google, Meta, Nvidia, and other major tech employers.

### Commercial Activity
Retail centered along Stevens Creek and De Anza corridors. Vallco redevelopment remains politically sensitive.

### Development Trends
Tear-down and rebuild activity dominates. ADU adoption increasing. Density growth remains constrained.

### Market Outlook
Long-term blue-chip residential ZIP with strong school-driven price resilience and limited volatility.

---

## 7. Safety

### Crime Profile
Low violent crime rates and below-average property crime relative to Santa Clara County.

### Neighborhood Risks
Occasional auto break-ins near commercial corridors; otherwise minimal safety concerns.

---

## 8. Schools & Education

### School Districts
Cupertino Union School District (elementary and middle)  
Fremont Union High School District (high school)

### High Schools
Cupertino High School  
Monta Vista High School (significant price premium driver)

### School Impact on Housing
School boundaries strongly influence pricing, with differences often exceeding $500,000 for similar homes.

---

## Summary Positioning
ZIP code 95014 represents one of the most stable, school-driven, and supply-constrained residential markets in Silicon Valley, favored for long-term capital preservation and family-oriented living.
