# ZIP Code Report: 95118 (San Jose – Cambrian Area)

## 1. Geological & Physical Setting
### 1.1 Geographic Position
ZIP 95118 lies in the southwestern portion of San Jose, commonly associated with the Cambrian area. It sits between Almaden Valley to the south, Willow Glen to the north, and Los Gatos/Campbell to the west.

### 1.2 Natural Boundaries & Topography
Terrain is gently sloped valley floor transitioning toward the foothills of Almaden Valley. Natural boundaries include Guadalupe River corridor and local creek drainage channels.

### 1.3 Land Use Patterns
Primarily residential (single‑family ranch homes), with neighborhood retail corridors along Camden Avenue, Branham Lane, and Almaden Expressway.

## 2. Climate & Environmental Hazards
### 2.1 Local Climate & Weather Patterns
Mediterranean climate: warm, dry summers; cool, wet winters. AQI generally Good–Moderate, PM2.5 being the most common pollutant.

### 2.2 Wildfire Exposure & Historical Fire Activity
Not directly in wildland–urban interface. Regional wildfire smoke impacts occur seasonally.

### 2.3 Flood, Stormwater, and Water‑Related Hazards
Some parcels sit within stormwater risk zones along creeks. Broader San Jose risk ranges indicate ~42% of structures have some level of flood risk.

### 2.4 Seismic Activity & Fault Proximity
Proximity to San Andreas and Calaveras fault systems (regional). Standard Bay Area seismic risk zone, but not uniquely high relative to the region.

### 2.5 Environmental Compliance, Superfund Sites & Pollution Indicators
No major Superfund sites within 95118. Regional air quality is occasionally affected by wildfire events.

## 3. Community & Demographics
### 3.1 Communities, Neighborhoods, and Subdivisions
Includes Cambrian Park–adjacent residential pockets such as Carlton, Kooser, Branham, and Almaden‑adjacent neighborhoods.

### 3.2 Population Size, Density & Demographic Composition
Population approx. 31.8k–32.8k. Density ~7.8k–8k residents per sq mi.  
Demographics: ~43% White, ~27% Hispanic, ~22% Asian.

### 3.3 Household Characteristics
Median age ~39. Median household income ~\$148k. Over half of residents hold a college degree or higher.

## 4. Housing & Neighborhood Character
### 4.1 Housing Stock Distribution & Architectural Character
Dominantly 1950s–1970s ranch‑style single‑family homes, typical sizes 1,200–1,700 sq ft. Some condo/townhome clusters 1,100–1,600 sq ft.

### 4.2 Median Home Prices, Trends & Affordability Metrics
Median sale price ~\$1.7M. Median list price ~\$1.5M. Zillow Home Value Index approx. \$1.60M. Homes typically go pending in ~2 weeks.

### 4.3 Income Distribution & Median Household Income
Upper‑middle‑income profile; median household income ~\$148k, above both state and national medians.

### 4.4 Rental Market Snapshot
Inventory relatively limited; rents higher than San Jose average due to school district desirability.

## 5. Commute & Access
### 5.1 Major Roadways, Transit Access & Walkability
Highway 85 bisects the ZIP; close to Highway 87. Transit access through VTA routes; nearest light‑rail/Caltrain stations include Blossom Hill, Tamien, Almaden.

### 5.2 Typical Commute Times to Key Employment Areas
Average commute ~30 minutes. Approx drive times:  
– Downtown SJ: 15–20 min  
– North San Jose/Cisco: 20–30 min  
– Cupertino/Apple Park: 20–25 min  
– Mountain View/Google: 30–40 min  

### 5.3 Infrastructure Constraints & Planned Improvements
Periodic congestion on 85 and 87. Improvements typically occur at the city/regional level.

## 6. Economic Development & Outlook
### 6.1 Major Employers Within or Near the ZIP Code
Nearby headquarters: eBay (95125), Adobe (Downtown SJ), Cisco (North SJ), Netflix (Los Gatos). Local employers mostly retail, education, and medical offices.

### 6.2 Local Business Environment & Commercial Activity
Primarily neighborhood‑serving retail. Strategic location between Campbell, Los Gatos, and San Jose supports continuous commercial demand.

### 6.3 Development Trends, Pipeline Projects & Market Outlook
Low inventory + strong school districts support long‑term value retention. Mostly infill remodels/expansions rather than large‑scale redevelopment.

### 6.4 Resident Sentiment & Public Opinion Indicators
General perception: safe, family‑friendly, good schools, strong commuter location.

## 7. Safety

Violent crime ~22.2 vs U.S. avg 22.7; property crime ~32.4 vs U.S. avg 35.4 — slightly safer than national averages.

### 7.2 Violent vs. Property Crime Rates
Property crime is the dominant category; violent crime relatively low for an urban ZIP.

### 7.3 Crime Trends & Notable Recent Incidents
CrimeGrade estimates ~19 incidents per 1,000 residents annually. No persistent hotspot patterns; varies by block.

### 7.4 Neighborhood-Level Safety Concerns
Isolated property crime clusters near commercial strips; interior residential streets tend to be quieter.
