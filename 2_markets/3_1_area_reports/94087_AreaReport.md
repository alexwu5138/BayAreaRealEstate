# ZIP Code 94087 — Sunnyvale, California
*Santa Clara County | West Valley | School-Driven Suburban Core Adjacent to Major Tech Campuses*

## 1. Geological & Physical Setting

### 1.1 Geographic Position
ZIP **94087** covers a large portion of **south-central and western Sunnyvale**, generally bounded by Cupertino (95014) to the south and southwest, Mountain View (94040/94041) to the northwest, and central Sunnyvale areas to the east. It sits squarely on the Santa Clara Valley floor with excellent regional connectivity.

### 1.2 Natural Boundaries & Topography
- Predominantly **flat alluvial terrain**, historically orchard land prior to mid‑20th‑century suburban development.
- No steep hillside or foothill terrain; lot usability is typically high.
- Local drainage is managed via engineered stormwater systems rather than natural creeks dominating lot design.

### 1.3 Land Use Patterns
- Primarily **single‑family residential**, especially in interior tracts.
- Pockets of townhomes, condos, and small multifamily developments near arterials.
- Commercial activity clusters along major corridors (Homestead Rd, El Camino Real adjacency, Hollenbeck Ave, Mathilda Ave influence depending on location).
- Largely **built‑out**, with change driven by remodels, rebuilds, and ADUs.

## 2. Climate & Environmental Hazards

### 2.1 Local Climate & Weather Patterns
Mediterranean valley climate: warm, dry summers and mild winters. Compared with Peninsula ZIPs, 94087 typically experiences **warmer daytime highs** and minimal marine-layer influence.

### 2.2 Wildfire Exposure & Historical Fire Activity
Wildfire risk is generally **low** relative to foothill and WUI communities. Address-level verification via county/state Fire Hazard Severity Zone (FHSZ) maps is still prudent but most interior tracts fall outside elevated hazard designations.

### 2.3 Flood, Stormwater, and Water-Related Hazards
Flood risk is typically **localized** and tied to stormwater capacity rather than mapped floodplains. Buyers should confirm FEMA flood zone status by address, but widespread flood risk is uncommon in this ZIP.

### 2.4 Seismic Activity & Fault Proximity
94087 carries the baseline Santa Clara Valley seismic profile. Practical diligence usually focuses on:
- Foundation type (raised vs slab-on-grade)
- Evidence of settlement or differential movement
- Retrofit history where applicable

### 2.5 Environmental Compliance & Legacy Issues
Sunnyvale, like much of Silicon Valley, has a history of electronics-industry groundwater remediation at certain sites. For residential properties in 94087, diligence is typically disclosure-driven and site-specific rather than ZIP-wide.

## 3. Community & Demographics

### 3.1 Communities, Neighborhoods, and Subdivisions
Neighborhood identity in 94087 is largely tract-based rather than named subdivisions. Buyers often distinguish:
- Interior single-family tracts (quiet, school-focused)
- Corridor-adjacent areas (more noise, higher density potential)
- Proximity to Cupertino border (often perceived as premium)

### 3.2 Population Size, Density & Demographic Composition
94087 has a **high owner-occupancy rate** relative to other Sunnyvale ZIPs, with a strong concentration of professional and technical households.

### 3.3 Household Characteristics
Common patterns include:
- Family households prioritizing schools
- Dual-income tech professionals
- Long-term ownership, contributing to low inventory turnover

## 4. Housing & Neighborhood Character

### 4.1 Housing Stock Distribution & Architectural Character
- Predominantly **1950s–1970s single-family ranch homes**
- Typical lot sizes ~5,500–7,500 sq ft
- Extensive remodeling, expansions, and tear-down/rebuild activity
- ADUs increasingly common where zoning allows

### 4.2 Pricing, Trends & Market Position (Directional)
94087 is widely viewed as a **premium Sunnyvale ZIP**, with pricing influenced by:
- School assignment (especially Cupertino Union / Fremont Union pathways)
- Interior-tract location versus arterial adjacency
- Remodel quality and permit history
- Lot usability and expansion potential

*(Numeric medians and trends can be provided on request using MLS/Redfin/Zillow data rather than estimates.)*

### 4.3 Income Distribution & Affordability
Household incomes are generally high, supported by proximity to major tech employers. Affordability remains constrained even for well-qualified buyers.

### 4.4 Rental Market Snapshot
Rental demand is strong for single-family homes and high-quality townhomes, driven by school access and commute convenience. Inventory is limited relative to demand.

## 5. Commute & Access

### 5.1 Major Roadways, Transit Access & Walkability
- Excellent access to **SR‑85, I‑280, US‑101** (distance varies by address)
- Arterial connectivity via Homestead Rd, De Anza Blvd, and Wolfe Rd
- Transit utility is moderate; most commutes remain car-based
- Walkability varies, highest near neighborhood retail and schools

### 5.2 Typical Commute Patterns (Directional)
- Apple Park (Cupertino): ~5–10 minutes
- Mountain View / Google campuses: ~10–20 minutes
- Santa Clara / North San José: short-to-moderate drive times
- SF commute: feasible but time-of-day sensitive; Caltrain access typically requires a drive

### 5.3 Infrastructure Constraints & Planned Improvements
Primary constraints are peak-hour congestion and school-hour traffic. Large-scale roadway expansion is unlikely due to built-out conditions.

## 8. Schools & Education

### 8.1 Elementary Schools
School assignment is **address-based** and varies within the ZIP. Portions of 94087 are served by:
- **Cupertino Union School District (CUSD)**
- **Sunnyvale School District (SSD)**  
Verification must be done by exact address.

### 8.2 Middle Schools
Middle school pathways depend on the elementary district and address; verification via official district tools is required.

### 8.3 High Schools
Most students attend high schools within **Fremont Union High School District (FUHSD)**, such as:
- Fremont High School
- Homestead High School  
Boundary verification is critical for valuation and offer strategy.

### 8.4 Private & Alternative Options
Private schools are available regionally, but public school assignment remains the dominant demand driver.

### 8.5 School Impact on Housing
School boundaries in 94087 can produce **substantial pricing differences** for otherwise similar homes. ZIP alone is not sufficient for school inference.

## Practical Due-Diligence Checklist (94087-specific)
1. **Schools:** verify elementary, middle, and FUHSD high school assignment by exact address.
2. **Flood check:** confirm FEMA flood zone status (generally low risk but address-specific).
3. **Home age risks:** evaluate electrical capacity, sewer laterals, and prior permit history for mid-century homes.
4. **Noise exposure:** assess proximity to major arterials versus interior streets.
5. **Lot potential:** confirm expansion/ADU feasibility under Sunnyvale zoning rules.

## Summary Positioning
ZIP code **94087** is a **core, school-driven Sunnyvale market** with flat, usable lots, strong tech adjacency, and chronically constrained supply. It is favored for long-term ownership, family living, and relative pricing resilience within Silicon Valley.
