# ZIP Code Community Report — 94517 (Clayton, CA)

> **Purpose & Scope**  
> This is a **high‑level, non‑technical area overview** intended for buyer orientation, listing context, and quick comparative analysis. It is not a substitute for formal disclosures, inspections, or jurisdiction‑specific reports.

---

## 1. Geological & Physical Setting

### 1.1 Geographic Position
ZIP code **94517** primarily covers the City of Clayton in eastern Contra Costa County. It sits at the eastern edge of the Diablo Valley, directly adjacent to Concord and at the foothills of Mount Diablo.

### 1.2 Natural Boundaries & Topography
The area transitions from relatively flat residential pockets into rolling hills and steeper terrain toward Mount Diablo State Park. Open space, ridgelines, and preserved land naturally limit outward expansion.

### 1.3 Land Use Patterns
Land use is dominated by low‑density residential neighborhoods. Commercial uses are limited and concentrated near downtown Clayton. There is little to no industrial zoning within the ZIP.

---

## 2. Climate & Environmental Hazards

### 2.1 Local Climate & Weather Patterns
94517 experiences a warm inland Mediterranean climate. Summers are hot and dry, often warmer than coastal Contra Costa areas. Winters are mild with seasonal rainfall.

### 2.2 Wildfire Exposure & Historical Fire Activity
Proximity to open space and foothill terrain places parts of 94517 in elevated wildfire risk categories. Buyers should review fire‑hardening features, defensible space, and insurance availability.

### 2.3 Flood, Stormwater, and Water‑Related Hazards
Flood risk is generally low due to elevation and terrain. Isolated drainage issues may exist in specific parcels but are not widespread.

### 2.4 Seismic Activity & Fault Proximity
As with much of the East Bay, the area is subject to regional seismic risk. Modern construction and retrofits are relevant considerations for risk mitigation.

### 2.5 Environmental Compliance & Pollution Indicators
There are no well‑known Superfund sites within the ZIP. Environmental quality is generally supported by limited commercial activity and significant surrounding open space.

---

## 3. Community & Demographics

### 3.1 Communities & Neighborhoods
The ZIP largely corresponds to Clayton itself, with neighborhoods consisting of established subdivisions and semi‑custom hillside developments.

### 3.2 Population & Density
Clayton is a small, low‑density city with a strong suburban character and a stable resident base.

### 3.3 Household Characteristics
Households are predominantly owner‑occupied, family‑oriented, and long‑tenured. Educational attainment and household stability are generally above county averages.

---

## 4. Housing & Neighborhood Character

### 4.1 Housing Stock & Architecture
Housing is overwhelmingly single‑family detached homes, many built from the 1970s through early 2000s. Architectural styles are primarily suburban California traditional.

### 4.2 Home Prices & Market Positioning
Home values tend to exceed the Contra Costa County average but remain below premium Lamorinda ZIP codes. Pricing reflects a balance of space, safety, and commute trade‑offs.

### 4.3 Income Profile
Median household income is upper‑middle range, supported by professional and dual‑income households commuting to regional job centers.

### 4.4 Rental Market
Rental inventory is limited and primarily consists of single‑family homes. Rental turnover is low, and supply constraints are common.

---

## 5. Commute & Access

### 5.1 Transportation & Roadways
Access is primarily via Clayton Road and connections to Treat Boulevard and Ygnacio Valley Road. Public transit options are limited; car dependency is the norm.

### 5.2 Commute Patterns
Commutes to Concord, Walnut Creek, and Pleasant Hill are relatively manageable. Longer commutes to Oakland or San Francisco are common for residents.

### 5.3 Infrastructure Constraints
Geography and open‑space protections limit large‑scale infrastructure expansion, reinforcing the area’s low‑growth profile.

---

## 6. Economic Development & Outlook

### 6.1 Employment Base
There are no major employment centers within the ZIP. Residents typically work in surrounding East Bay cities.

### 6.2 Local Business Environment
Downtown Clayton supports small‑scale dining, retail, and service businesses with a strong community focus.

### 6.3 Development Trends
New development is limited by zoning, geography, and resident preferences. Market behavior favors stability over rapid change.

### 6.4 Resident Sentiment
Community sentiment strongly favors preservation, safety, and controlled growth.

---

## 7. Safety

### 7.1 Crime Overview
Overall crime levels are lower than county averages, particularly for violent crime.

### 7.2 Crime Composition
Property crime exists but is generally sporadic rather than systemic.

### 7.3 Trends
No persistent negative crime trends define the ZIP.

### 7.4 Localized Considerations
Primary non‑crime risk considerations relate to wildfire exposure in hillside areas.

Public schools are generally served through Mount Diablo Unified School District, with local attendance boundaries applying.

### 8.4 Alternative Education Options
Charter and private school options exist within driving distance.
