# ZIP Code 94563 — Orinda, California
*Contra Costa County | Lamorinda | East Bay Hills / Highway 24 Corridor*

## 1. Geological & Physical Setting

### 1.1 Geographic Position
ZIP code **94563** primarily corresponds to **Orinda**, an East Bay community in Contra Costa County along the **SR‑24 corridor**, roughly between the Oakland/Berkeley hills and Walnut Creek. It is part of the broader “Lamorinda” submarket (Lafayette–Moraga–Orinda).

### 1.2 Natural Boundaries & Topography
Orinda is characterized by **rolling hills, steep ridgelines, and narrow valleys** typical of the inner Coast Ranges. The area includes:
- **Hillside residential tracts** with winding roads and significant grade changes
- **Valley-floor pockets** near the Orinda BART/Downtown area
- Multiple **drainages and creeks** that can influence lot usability, retaining needs, and stormwater behavior

### 1.3 Land Use Patterns
- Predominantly **single-family residential** with low density
- Small commercial core near **Orinda Village / Orinda BART**
- Open-space adjacency and hillside/wildland interface conditions are common, shaping landscaping, defensible space, and rebuild requirements

## 2. Climate & Environmental Hazards

### 2.1 Local Climate & Weather Patterns
Mediterranean climate: warm, dry summers and mild, wetter winters. Compared with Bay-adjacent areas, Orinda tends to have **warmer summer afternoons** and **cooler nights** due to topography and inland location.

### 2.2 Wildfire Exposure & Historical Fire Activity
Wildfire is a **primary risk driver** for 94563 due to:
- **Steep terrain**, **fuel continuity**, and proximity to wildland areas
- Presence of **Very High Fire Severity Zone (VHFSZ)** / WUI designations in portions of the city (property-specific)

Practical implications:
- Defensible space and vegetation management expectations
- Higher insurance scrutiny and potential underwriting constraints (carrier-dependent)
- Remodel/new construction may trigger additional ignition-resistant construction standards if located within designated fire zones

### 2.3 Flood, Stormwater, and Water-Related Hazards
Flood risk is generally **localized** rather than widespread:
- Properties near creeks/valley floors can have mapped or functional flood/drainage constraints
- Many hillside lots require careful stormwater conveyance planning to prevent slope/erosion impacts

For property diligence: confirm the address against FEMA flood maps and/or California floodplain viewers.

### 2.4 Seismic Activity & Fault Proximity
Orinda’s hillside setting brings typical Bay Area seismic exposure, with **strong shaking potential** and localized slope/landslide sensitivity on steeper sites. Practical considerations include:
- Foundation type and retrofits (cripple wall bracing/bolting where applicable)
- Retaining walls, drainage, and hillside stability controls

### 2.5 Environmental Compliance, Superfund Sites & Pollution Indicators
Orinda is not commonly characterized by heavy industrial land use. Most environmental diligence for residential real estate in 94563 is oriented around:
- **Hillside/wildland** constraints (grading, vegetation, defensible space)
- **Creek setbacks and drainage controls**
- Any site-specific disclosures for past soil disturbance, underground storage tanks, or significant grading history
